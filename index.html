01 / The frame

The bottleneck is not hiring. It is how the spec moves.

Most firms moving up-market misread the problem as a hiring problem. At Innodata's scale, the actual bottleneck sits one layer above the delivery tooling. Context moves from the customer conversation, through the account team, into operations, and out to a specialist in Chennai or Manila who starts annotating tomorrow morning. That handoff is a specification-management problem. Solvable. The work is methodology, not software.

About 60% of what a large data-engineering operation does every day is database and code work. About 30% is rule-based logic. Around 10% is a genuine AI problem. The work is sorting those three into the right layers before anything is built. Applied to Innodata's stack the map resolves fast.

Spec propagation and change tracking across 5,000 specialists is a database and workflow problem. Quality-gate logic and appropriateness review for a new program like Rodeo is rule-based. Translating tacit senior-annotator judgment into reusable context that a new hire can pick up on day three is where an AI layer earns its keep.

03 / The underlying research

The methodology is published.

Interpretable Context Methodology: Folder Structure as Agent Architecture. Submitted to ACM TiiS. MIT licensed. The paper treats agent context as a layered filesystem with measurable interpretability and reproducibility gains. Applied to Innodata, it is the scaffold for how a spec written once in the customer conversation survives the trip to an annotator on a new program without degrading.

github.com/RinDig/Interpretable-Context-Methodology-ICM-

Companion paper: Ethics Engine (arXiv:2510.11742). A psychometric assessment tool for evaluating ideological and moral patterns in LLMs. Relevant for any program that has to stand up to external review (SHIELD, Rodeo, regulated-industry buyers).
